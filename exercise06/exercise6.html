Brief about the Children's Day

​Children's Day is a global observance dedicated to promoting the rights and well-being of children. The concept originated in the 19th century, with Reverend Dr. Charles Leonard holding a special service for children in 1856. Internationally, the United Nations established Universal Children's Day on November 20, 1954, to promote children's rights and welfare.

In Ghana, Children's Day is celebrated on November 20 each year. The Evangelical Presbyterian Church, Ghana (EPCG), aligns with this national observance by organizing activities that emphasize the spiritual, educational, and social development of children within the church community. These celebrations typically include special church services, educational programs, and recreational activities designed to honor and nurture the younger members of the congregation.​

The EPCG's commitment to Children's Day reflects a broader dedication to fostering a supportive environment for children, ensuring they receive guidance and opportunities to grow in their faith and personal development.​

This year, the Children's Day celebration took place on March 16, 2025, at the Accra International Conference Center. The event was attended by children, their parents, guardians, and other family members, along with the Children’s Service teachers who diligently prepared the children for the occasion.

The Children’s Ministry consists of various departments, including the Prayer Force, Drama Troupe, Choreography Group, Bible Quiz Group, Sword Drill Team, and Memory Verse Boxing Team, among others. Currently, five teachers oversee the children's service: Mr. Michael Opong, Mrs. Lucy Adjei, Miss Jemima Anang, and Mr. Daniel Quaye.
